Potato scab disease

Name of the disease:

This disease is caused by the attack of bacteria called Sterptomyces scabies .

Symptoms:

  • Mild ringworm causes raised and raised brown spots of various sizes on the ubber.
  • Deep ringworm causes round pits or pits.
  • Blight disease causes brown crusty spots of various sizes on the potato. If the attack is severe, the entire potato becomes covered with spots and sometimes the spots die off.
  • Disease attack is usually limited to the skin. High temperature and high humidity are conducive to the spread of disease.
  • This disease is seed and soil borne. Without a host plant, the pathogen can survive in the soil for more than five (5) years.
  • Generally, the disease is more common if there is not enough juice in the ground for at least 30 days when the potato comes to the plant or if there is excess juice in the ground after the age of the potato plant is 65 days.
  • The germs of this disease survive for a long time in different types of crops such as radish, carrot, turnip.

Integrated Suppression Management:

  • Use disease free seeds.
  • Reducing excessive use of urea in land.
  • Mixing boric acid 3 grams + 1 liter of water to treat the seeds or apply it on the seeds.
  • Mixing 1 liter of water and using 120 kg of gypsum fertilizer per hectare for seed treatment.
  • Potatoes infected with this disease should never be used as seed potatoes.
  • Use balanced fertilizers.
  • Variations in irrigation often trigger tooth decay. In order to control rust disease, it should be observed that there is no shortage of sap in the soil for 30-35 days after planting potatoes.
